# Sunny Shore

Sunny Shore is the first-world and vertical-slice candidate for Shellbound.

It is a compact chain of small sunny islands connected by bridges, docks, and old beam machinery. The player starts on a quiet arrival islet, works across the island chain, rings the bell in a giant shore tower, raises a drawbridge, and reaches the Great Red Lobstrocity guarding the broken beam.

The world should feel cozy, readable, salty, and a little strange.

## Canonical Name

Sunny Shore is singular.

Do not use Sunny Shores as the canonical name.

## Core Flow

1. Player beams into the Arrival Islet.
2. Camera framing and landmarks show the broken beam, distant bell tower, blocked drawbridge, and island chain.
3. Player crosses small islands that teach one action at a time.
4. Midpoint Dock introduces Shellvis and Grunk after the player has collected some junk.
5. Player reaches the Bell Tower Courtyard.
6. Player rings the tower bell.
7. The Sunny Shore bell gate/drawbridge cutaway plays.
8. The raised drawbridge opens the final route.
9. Player reaches the Final Reef and encounters the Great Red Lobstrocity.
10. Resolving the encounter restores the beam.
11. Sunny Shore completion unlocks the Sailor Cap cosmetic reward.

## Arrival Islet

The player beams into a small safe island with a clear view of the world.

Visible from arrival:

- Distant bell tower
- Broken beam glow
- Raised or blocked drawbridge
- First bridge route
- A few pieces of junk
- The first Beam Anchor

Design intent:

- Give the player a calm first step into the world.
- Let landmarks explain the goal before dialogue does.
- Make the bell tower feel like the obvious destination.

## Island Chain

The island chain is a compact movement sampler.

Each island should teach one core interaction through layout:

- Jump gaps between docks or rocks
- Shell Dash through brittle beach junk or cracked driftwood
- Use the slingshot to hit a switch, pulley, or bridge latch
- Activate a Beam Anchor as a checkpoint and restoration object

The route should be readable from a distance, with optional side pockets for junk and small secrets.

## Shellvis and Grunk

Shellvis and Grunk appear at Midpoint Dock.

The player should reach them after collecting enough junk for the economy loop to matter.

Shellvis:

- Operates a dockside stall
- Previews Sunny Shore cosmetics
- Should visibly suggest the Sailor Cap reward
- Does not explain how he arrived

Grunk:

- Operates near a harbor junk pile
- Exchanges junk for Grunk
- Only says "Grunk"
- Does not explain how Grunk arrived

Nobody comments on their presence.

## Beam Anchors

Sunny Shore has four Beam Anchors:

- Arrival Islet Anchor
- Midpoint Dock Anchor
- Bell Tower Courtyard Anchor
- Final Reef / Lobstrocity Approach Anchor

Each anchor should support progress, respawn, and completion tracking.

The anchors should become cleaner, brighter, and more connected after activation.

## Bell Tower And Drawbridge

The Bell Tower is the main visible destination for the first half of Sunny Shore.

Ringing the bell raises the drawbridge.

The bell is not the final beam restoration act. It opens the route to the final encounter.

The bell interaction should trigger the existing Sunny Shore bell gate/drawbridge cutaway:

- Show the tower bell or bell mechanism
- Show the drawbridge raising
- Use the VHS cutaway style
- Return control quickly
- Let the player skip or replay through the existing cutaway rules

The bell tower should feel like old shore infrastructure tied to the beam network, not a lore lecture.

## Great Red Lobstrocity

The Great Red Lobstrocity is a boss-like guardian gate for the first pass.

It is not a full multi-phase boss fight yet.

Role:

- Blocks the final beam route
- Tests movement, dodging, and basic encounter readability
- Speaks in fragmented shore-memory language
- Guards the beam/tower mechanism because it remembers a broken promise

Design notes:

- The player should understand what to do without a long explanation.
- The encounter should feel memorable before it feels difficult.
- Resolving the encounter restores the beam.
- Do not fully explain what promise was broken.

Possible phrases:

- "Red remember."
- "Bell said stay."
- "Beam broke promise."
- "Not wave. Walker."

## Completion Reward

Completing Sunny Shore unlocks the Sailor Cap cosmetic.

The Sailor Cap is expression-only.

It should not increase power, damage, health, movement speed, or economy value.

Shellvis can preview the Sailor Cap through his Sunny Shore outfit or stall display before the player earns it.

## Hub Change

After Sunny Shore is completed, The Great Shell should show a small visible change.

Possible directions:

- A beach decoration appears
- A Sunny Shore traveler arrives
- Shellvis adds Sunny Shore-themed stock
- A restored beam glow becomes visible from the hub

Keep the hub change small for the first pass.

## Optional Weirdness

Sunny Shore should make surface-level sense as a beach adventure.

Players who look deeper may notice:

- Shellvis and Grunk are already here
- The bell tower machinery feels older than the beach around it
- Lobstrocity remembers something nobody explains
- The beam seems connected to ordinary seaside infrastructure

Do not turn these details into required lore homework.
