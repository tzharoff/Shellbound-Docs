# Enemies

## Enemy Philosophy

Enemies should support movement, traversal, and readable play. They should be obstacles and personalities, not stat checks.

Good enemy design should:
- Be easy to understand quickly.
- Create movement decisions.
- Fit the world identity.
- Leave room for avoidance, combat, or clever traversal.

## Standard Enemies

Standard enemies are local threats that make worlds feel alive.

Open needs:
- Sunny Shore enemy set
- Beam Forest enemy set
- Endless Tracks enemy set

## Elite Enemies

Elite enemies should be rare enough to feel memorable.

Potential roles:
- Guard secrets
- Teach mechanics
- Add tension before boss spaces

## Boss Design

Bosses should feel like strange world anchors, not just health bars.

Known bosses:
- Great Red Lobstrocity
- Iron Bear

Bosses can reveal deeper lore through fragmented speech, visual behavior, and what changes after they leave.

## Difficulty Progression

Difficulty should grow through:
- More demanding traversal
- More layered enemy patterns
- More interesting spaces
- Better use of learned movement

Avoid:
- Health sponge enemies
- Required damage upgrades
- Stat-gated combat
