# Elevator Pitch

## One Sentence Pitch

Shellbound is a nostalgic PS1-inspired third-person adventure collectathon where a small turtle restores ancient beams, gathers valuable junk, and searches strange connected worlds for the Great Turtle.

## Short Description

Explore colorful worlds, collect junk, restore ancient beams, and trade hard-earned Grunk for cosmetics, shell styles, dash effects, and hub decorations.

Shellbound is cozy on the surface, odd around the edges, and quietly full of things that remember more than they should.

## Full Description

Players control a small turtle who leaves home after a mysterious disappearance and discovers that distant worlds are no longer connected like they once were.

By restoring forgotten beam pathways, the turtle travels across beaches, mechanical forests, candy-colored highlands, forgotten aquariums, temples, and impossible places with too many shells under them.

Movement is inspired by classic 3D platformers:
- Expressive jumping
- Shell dashing
- Slingshot traversal and combat
- Exploration-first level design

Each world introduces new enemies, traversal ideas, collectibles, and small pieces of a larger mystery.

Along the way, players collect junk, bring it to Grunk, receive Grunk, and spend it with Shellvis, a suspicious merchant in an obviously fake turtle costume.

As worlds reconnect, NPC dialogue changes, memories drift, and strange details begin appearing:
- Bosses speak in half-understood phrases.
- Some characters seem to remember previous journeys.
- Time becomes unreliable.
- Worlds feel older than they should.

Most players will experience Shellbound as a cozy retro adventure.

Players who look closer may discover something stranger.

Because beneath every world, there may be another shell.
